Mentorship Circles

JWPA is building a structured mentorship program exclusively for member physicians — connecting women doctors across specialties and career stages for ongoing guidance, peer community, and career support.

Express Interest Join JWPA First
The Vision

What Mentorship Circles will look like

Mentorship is at the core of what JWPA was built to do. Every woman physician — whether she's in her first year of practice or a 20-year attending — benefits from a community of peers and mentors who understand exactly what she's navigating.

JWPA Mentorship Circles will create that community in a structured, intentional way. Exclusively for JWPA member physicians, the program will connect women across career stages and specialties for ongoing conversations, guidance, and support.

The program is currently being developed by Dr. Nahar and the JWPA leadership team. If you're a member interested in participating — as a mentor, a mentee, or both — register your interest below and we'll reach out when the program launches.

Cross-specialty connections

Circles will span specialties intentionally — so a GI fellow learns from a surgeon, and a medical student hears from a pediatrician and a hospitalist.

Ongoing, not one-time

Circles will be ongoing relationships — not a single meeting or event. The goal is sustained community, not a checkbox.

JWPA members only

The program will be exclusive to active JWPA members — a meaningful benefit of being part of the community.

Structured guidance built in

JWPA will provide conversation frameworks and resources so circles have direction — not just open-ended meetings with no agenda.

Who It's For

Open to all JWPA members

Mentorship Circles will serve members at every stage — whether you're looking for guidance, want to give back, or both.

Early-Career Physicians

Attending physicians earlier in their careers who want guidance on navigating hospital systems, building a practice, and growing into leadership roles.

Experienced Physicians

Senior attending physicians who want to give back by mentoring colleagues — and who benefit from peer community with other experienced women in medicine.
